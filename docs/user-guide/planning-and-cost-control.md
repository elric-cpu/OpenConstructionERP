# Planning and cost control

An estimate says what a job should cost. Planning and cost control track what it is actually costing and when, so surprises show up as trends you can act on rather than as a shock at the end. This is where the schedule, the cost model and the change record come together.

## What it is for

The workflow answers three questions on a live project: are we on time, are we on budget, and where are we heading. It links the priced work to a timeline so cost has a shape over time, measures progress against the plan, forecasts the outturn, and keeps a disciplined record of the changes and risks that move the numbers.

## Schedule the work (4D)

The 4D schedule places BOQ work on a timeline. You build a Gantt chart with activities, dependencies of all four types and a highlighted critical path, or you generate activities directly from BOQ sections with durations proportional to their cost. For teams that run lean delivery, the advanced scheduling module adds Last Planner working: phase plans, look-ahead planning, constraint logs, weekly work plans, commitments, and percent-plan-complete tracking with reasons for non-completion, all against baselines with delta tracking.

## Track the cost (5D)

The 5D cost model spreads the priced BOQ across the schedule to produce S-curves, cash-flow projections, budgets and earned value. From there the earned-value tools report the standard indices for schedule and cost performance, estimate at completion, and to-complete performance, with forecasting and S-curves for the whole project. Cost-value reconciliation and cash-flow views compare value earned against cost incurred, and finance handles the invoices, payments and budgets underneath.

## Control the change

Cost control is mostly change control. The platform tracks change orders, variations and site measurements, and management-of-change entries, each with its cost and schedule impact. A change-intelligence layer reads across that whole family to tell you what is waiting on whom and for how long. A risk register scores probability against impact, supports Monte Carlo analysis for a risk-adjusted contingency, and links risks to the estimate. Cost recovery records back-charges you intend to recover from the party that caused them.

## See it in one place

An executive project-controls dashboard puts cost, schedule, quality, safety, risk and change key indicators side by side, currency-honest, with one-click drill-down to the module that owns each number. Progress tracking, dashboards and the reporting tools turn the underlying data into the snapshots and reports a project review needs.

## The steps

1. Build or generate the schedule from the BOQ, set the dependencies, and confirm the critical path.
2. Baseline the plan and the budget so you have something to measure against.
3. As work proceeds, record progress and actual cost, and let the earned-value and cash-flow views update.
4. Log changes, variations and risks as they arise, with their cost and time impact, and watch the forecast at completion move.
5. Review the project-controls dashboard, drill into anything that has turned amber or red, and report out.
