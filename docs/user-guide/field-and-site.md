# Field and site operations

Most of a project's real history is made on site, and most disputes are lost for want of a record of it. The field and site tools capture what happened, day by day, in a form that stands up later: who was there, what was delivered, what was inspected, what went wrong, and what was done about it.

## What it is for

The purpose is a complete, contemporaneous record and the daily control that produces it. That record protects the project commercially, feeds progress and cost back to the office, and turns site knowledge into data the rest of the platform can use. It is written for the site engineer and the foreman as much as for the project manager.

## The daily record

The daily site diary is the backbone. Entries pull the weather automatically from the project location and capture the crew on site, the equipment in use, deliveries, delays and photographs. Field time records the hours worked, and forms and checklists cover the recurring paperwork such as permits, briefings and pour cards. Voice capture lets someone dictate an entry from the field instead of typing it.

## Quality on site

Quality inspections manage the checks that gate the work, from concrete pours and waterproofing to mechanical and electrical and handover, each with a checklist and a pass or fail outcome. The punch list, or snag list, tracks defects from discovery through resolution, verification and close, with location pins on the drawing and photo evidence, and it insists that a different person verifies a fix from the one who made it. Non-conformance reports handle material, workmanship and design non-conformances with root-cause and corrective-action fields. For teams that want it unified, a broader construction-control workflow ties acceptance criteria, inspection regimes, material records and as-built evidence into hold, witness and review gates.

## Safety

Safety and HSE cover incident reporting and observations with risk scoring and corrective actions, and the advanced HSE module adds job safety analysis, permit to work, toolbox talks, audits, corrective and preventive actions, and the recognised safety performance indicators. Incidents can be plotted on the map for a portfolio-level view of where safety events cluster.

## Logistics and resources

Site logistics plans what arrives: access gates with operating hours and slot capacity, laydown zones, and a delivery booking board that validates each window against gate hours and rejects clashes, so two deliveries never fight for the same gate. Equipment and fleet management tracks the plant on site, and the accommodation module runs worker camps, rentals and visitor lodging in one data model.

## Communication and handover

The tools that keep the paper trail straight live here too: requests for information, submittals, transmittals, correspondence, a phone log, and meeting minutes with action items. A client and partner portal gives outside parties a controlled view, for example to review documents or submit against a tender, without access to the rest of the project. At the end, the handover and closeout workflow assembles the completion package from the punch list, inspections and document records into a single, verifiable deliverable.

## The steps

1. Set up the site tools you need on the project, at minimum the daily diary, inspections, punch list and safety.
2. Each day, record the diary entry, log field time, run the inspections that are due, and raise any snags or incidents with photos and location.
3. Book deliveries against the gate schedule, and keep the communication records, RFIs, submittals and correspondence, up to date.
4. Feed progress back so [planning and cost control](./planning-and-cost-control.md) reflects reality, and assemble the closeout package as the work completes.

## How it connects

Field records are not a dead end. Progress and actuals feed [planning and cost control](./planning-and-cost-control.md), quantities confirmed on site can update the [BOQ](./estimating-and-boq.md), snags and inspections roll into the closeout package, and safety and diary pins surface on the project map. The contemporaneous record also underpins claims and disputes, where the platform can assemble an ordered, reproducible evidence pack from the project's own timeline.
