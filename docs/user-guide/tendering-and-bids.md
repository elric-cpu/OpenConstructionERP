# Tendering and bid comparison

Once the scope is priced, most of it goes out to subcontractors and suppliers. Tendering turns a priced estimate into bid packages, gets them to the right companies, collects what comes back, and lays the offers side by side so you can award with confidence.

## What it is for

The workflow closes the loop between your estimate and the market. You carve the BOQ into packages that match how the work will actually be let, send each package to a shortlist with the scope and instructions attached, and gather the responses in one place. When the bids are in, you compare them line by line against each other and against your own priced base, so an unusually high or low number stands out instead of hiding in a total.

## When to use it

Use it when you are ready to buy the work, whether that is a main-contract tender to a client or a subcontract enquiry from a contractor to its trades. The same machinery serves a formal request for quotation and a lighter invitation to a few known firms.

## The steps

1. Build a bid package from the priced BOQ. Choose the sections and positions in scope, add instructions and any scope notes, and keep your own rates as the internal benchmark.
2. Distribute the package to your shortlist. Invitations can go out by email and through the client and partner portal, where recipients see only what you share with them.
3. Handle questions and submissions as they arrive. Bidders ask questions, you answer, and they submit priced returns against your package.
4. Compare and level the bids. Read them side by side in a price mirror, spot anomalies and gaps in coverage, and normalise the offers so you are comparing like with like.
5. Award. Pick the winner, and let any resulting scope adjustments flow through as change orders.

## How it connects

Tendering draws its scope and its benchmark rates from the [bill of quantities](./estimating-and-boq.md) and the [cost bases](./world-cost-bases.md). Packages export in open formats including GAEB XML for standards-based exchange. The people and companies you invite live in the shared contacts directory and subcontractor and supplier records, and the [client and partner portal](./field-and-site.md) is how outside parties take part without access to the rest of the project. After award, the commitment feeds [planning and cost control](./planning-and-cost-control.md), where the bought values become part of the cost picture.

## Related capabilities

Alongside the core tendering module, the platform includes a fuller bid-management workflow with invitations, question and answer threads, submissions, levelling and an award step, a request-for-quotation workflow for supplier pricing, and vendor and supplier catalog management. Procurement then carries the awarded work into purchase orders and goods receipts.
